How this works

Two streams of data, one map.

Sensors
Always-on measurement
Open-hardware environmental sensors across Bali — Smart Citizen Kits and others — measure air quality, noise, temperature and humidity continuously. Anyone in Bali with a compatible sensor can register theirs.
Citizen reports
From the people who live here
Residents tell us what they see, smell, hear or are worried about — burning trash, smoke, water issues, noise. Every report is reviewed by Fab Lab Bali before it appears on the map.
Community-defined
Sensors come after the question
Before measuring anything, we ask Bali residents which environmental issues matter most. The campaign is shaped by those answers. Take the survey →
Who leads · where the methodology comes from

Local lead, credited methodology.

Fab Lab Bali leads the campaign — local, accountable, run by people who live here. The methodology comes from the EU Making Sense project (2015–2017, Fab Lab Barcelona / IAAC). Bali is its own place; the community here is the co-author of what this becomes.

What the survey asks

Eight questions.

  1. What environmental issue do you most want made visible?
  2. Where? (neighborhood, road, school, beach)
  3. Who is most affected?
  4. What evidence already exists?
  5. What would change if this were visible — and to whom?
  6. Want to take part further? (host a sensor, report, translate, attend workshops)
  7. Preferred languages.
  8. Optional contact.
