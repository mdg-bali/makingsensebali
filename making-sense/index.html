What does your neighborhood want made visible?

Making Sense Bali is a sensing campaign built backwards from the question that matters: what environmental issue do you, your family, your school, your community most want to surface — and to whom? Sensors come second. The campaign comes from you. Fab Lab Bali is the local host and accountable institution.

What this is

Sensing as community practice, not as a dashboard.

Smart Citizen Bali is a campaign of Fab Lab Bali — the local fabrication laboratory and maker community, registered and operating in Bali, Indonesia. It pairs open-hardware environmental sensors with a citizen reporting system to make local environmental conditions visible. Two Smart Citizen Kits are already running, with the data live and public.

But sensors alone are not a sensing campaign. The data only matters if it answers a question the community is actually asking. So we're starting where the EU Making Sense project taught us to start — by collecting matters of concern from the people who live here, before deciding what to measure or how to report it.

Fab Lab Bali invites residents, schools, neighborhood associations (banjar), NGOs, and concerned individuals to tell us what you would want measured, where, and why. Your answers shape the campaign: which sensors get deployed, which categories the citizen reporting tools accept, which languages the platform is built in, and what response loops are designed for the data. As the local host, Fab Lab Bali is accountable for how this work is conducted, how data is handled, and how the community is engaged.

Who leads this · where the methodology comes from

Local lead, credited methodology.

The campaign is led by Fab Lab Bali — the local fab lab and maker community in Bali, part of the global Fab Lab network of more than 2,500 labs in over 100 countries. Fab Lab Bali is the accountable institution: based in Indonesia, run by people who live here, holding the relationships with schools, neighborhood associations, NGOs, and local government that make a citizen-sensing campaign legitimate and sustainable.

The methodology lifts from the EU Making Sense project (2015–2017), led by Fab Lab Barcelona / IAAC with partners across Amsterdam, Barcelona, and Pristina. Making Sense developed and tested a participatory framework for citizen-led environmental sensing — anchored in the principle that communities sustain what they helped define, and that sensors without a matter of concern produce data without consequence.

Fab Lab Bali is using the methodology, the open hardware (Smart Citizen Kit, born at Fab Lab Barcelona), and the lessons learned. We are not reusing the past projects — Bali is a different bioregion with different concerns, different languages, different institutions, and different politics around data and visibility. The community here is the co-author of what this becomes.

The campaign, in seven phases

The work, visible.

Citizen-sensing campaigns work when each phase is named and visible. The Making Sense methodology defines seven. Where we are right now — Phase 1 — is the most consequential: we cannot decide what to measure until we hear what matters.

01
Scoping
Collect matters of concern from the community. Decide what this campaign is about.
02
Community
Convene contributors — schools, fab labs, neighborhoods, individuals — around the chosen concern.
03
Planning
Co-design the sensing campaign. Decide what to measure, where, and how often.
04
Sensing
Deploy sensors and the citizen-reporting bot. Collect data over agreed timeframe.
05
Awareness
Read the data together — workshops, presentations — translate signals into shared understanding.
06
Action
Community decides response: visibility, advocacy, behavior change, institutional engagement.
07
Reflection
What worked, what didn't, what would we do differently. Document for the next bioregion.

Phase 1 · matters of concern

What should we make visible?

Below are starting categories for environmental concerns we've heard mentioned in Bali. They are prompts, not constraints. Tell us what's missing, what's ranked wrongly, and what specific places, times, or events you'd want a sensor or a citizen report to capture.

●
Burning waste
Open trash burning, agricultural burning, smoke and ash from neighboring properties.
●
Air quality
Dust, vehicle exhaust, kitchen smoke, construction particulate matter.
●
Water
River, beach, well, irrigation. Contamination, smells, color, plastic, runoff after rain.
●
Noise
Construction, traffic, venues, generators, late-night events. Sleep disruption.
●
Heat & humidity
Indoor and outdoor heat stress. Mold and damp in homes and schools.
●
Plastic & solid waste
Visible accumulation, dumping sites, post-rain plastic in waterways.
●
Soil & agriculture
Pesticides, soil quality, runoff, traditional vs industrial agricultural impacts.
●
Light pollution
Excessive lighting, advertising signage, impact on sleep, wildlife, sky visibility.
●
Other / your own
Anything we haven't named that matters to you. Tell us.
Take the survey

Tell us what matters.

The survey takes about 5 minutes. Available in English and Bahasa Indonesia (translation in progress — for now, the form is bilingual where possible). Your answers shape what gets measured, what the citizen-reporting bot accepts, and which partners we approach next.

You don't need any technical knowledge to participate. We are specifically looking for residents, parents, students, teachers, neighborhood leaders, fab lab members, NGO workers, and anyone who lives or works in Bali.

What the survey asks

Eight questions, five minutes.

If you'd like to see the questions before opening the form:

  1. What environmental issue would you most want to make visible? (open answer + categories)
  2. Where? (specific neighborhood, beach, road, school, time of day if relevant)
  3. Who is most affected? (you, your family, students, workers, the elderly, animals)
  4. What evidence already exists? (your own observations, others' reports, news, official data)
  5. What would change if data made this visible — and to whom?
  6. Are you willing to participate further? (host a sensor, share reports, attend workshops, translate)
  7. Preferred languages for the platform (Bahasa Indonesia, English, Bahasa Bali, other)
  8. Optional contact (only if you want to be invited to next steps)
Get involved · with Fab Lab Bali

Beyond the survey.

I have a sensor I'd like to add to the network

If you have a Smart Citizen Kit, a PurpleAir, a Sensor.Community device, or any other open-data environmental sensor in Bali — Fab Lab Bali wants to add you to the campaign dashboard. Reach out with your sensor ID and approximate location.

I represent a school, NGO, or community group

Schools, banjar (neighborhood associations), and community groups are central to Phase 2 (community building) and Phase 5 (awareness). If you're interested in hosting a sensor, running a workshop, or co-designing the campaign with Fab Lab Bali, please reach out.

I want to translate or localize

The platform needs Bahasa Indonesia from day one and Bahasa Bali if there's interest. Translation and cultural review are load-bearing work — Fab Lab Bali is specifically looking for local contributors here.
